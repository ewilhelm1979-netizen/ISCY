# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to making participation in the ISCY community a respectful, professional, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience level, or role.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive community include:

- communicating with respect and empathy
- giving and receiving constructive technical feedback
- focusing criticism on ideas, code, architecture, and evidence rather than individuals
- acknowledging mistakes and correcting them transparently
- respecting different levels of experience and different perspectives
- protecting confidential or security-sensitive information

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, intimidation, threats, or discriminatory language
- personal attacks, insults, or deliberately disruptive conduct
- publishing private or identifying information without permission
- pressuring others to disclose confidential information
- knowingly making false claims about security findings or contributors
- public disclosure of unresolved vulnerabilities contrary to the project's security policy

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other communication associated with ISCY. It also applies when someone is publicly representing the project.

## Good-Faith Security Research

Good-faith security research conducted in accordance with [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) is welcome. Security reports should remain private until coordinated disclosure is agreed upon.
